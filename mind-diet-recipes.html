Cookable by construction

Plenty of nutrition advice fails at the stove: “eat more leafy greens” isn't dinner. Vittelio closes that gap structurally — the app can only recommend a dish that has an authored recipe. It isn't a coverage promise that might slip; it's a rule built into the suggestion engine itself. If a dish appears on a card, the recipe is already there.

Built for actual cooking

  • One step at a time. Each step fills the screen with just what you need now — the sub-steps, the ingredients for that step, and a tip where it helps.
  • Real amounts. Ingredients come with practical measures (with metric alongside), and oven temperatures are given in Fahrenheit first.
  • Honest numbers. Cook times come from the actual steps, and per-serving calories are estimates from USDA food data — labeled as estimates, not lab results.

Cooking for more than one

Tell Vittelio how many people usually share your meals and every recipe scales its amounts to match — while batch dishes like a pot of chili stay honest batch recipes with a declared yield, so the shopping list buys the right amount either way. Dishes you love can be favorited and stay with you even across plan changes.

Finishing a recipe logs the meal

When you cook a plan recipe to the end, the meal logs itself and your live score moves — cooking is the tracking. There is no separate logging chore afterwards.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

Are the recipes difficult?

No — they are weeknight recipes, typically a handful of steps with common techniques. The cook view walks you through one step at a time, so nothing depends on kitchen experience.

Do I need special ingredients?

The recipes are built from ordinary grocery-store ingredients, and your weekly shopping list gathers exactly what your plan’s recipes use, aisle by aisle.

Can I scale recipes for my family?

Yes. Set how many people you cook for (up to six) and recipe amounts scale to match; batch dishes keep their honest pot-sized yield with leftovers covering the week.
